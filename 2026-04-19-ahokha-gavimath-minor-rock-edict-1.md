# Gāvimaṭh (Koppal) version of Ashoka's Minor Rock Edict No. 1

**Pali Text**  
**English Translation**

---

**Devānamppiyo āha**  
**Beloved of the Gods (Devānamppiyo) spoke thus:**

**Sātirekāni aḍḍhateyyāni vassāni yadā homi upāsako**  
More than two and a half years have passed since I became a lay devotee (*upāsaka*).

**na ca kho bāḷhaṃ pakatā**  
But indeed I was not very zealous at first.

**saṃvacchare sātireko yadā me saṅghe upāsito, bāḷhaṃ ca me pakatā**  
Only after more than a year, when I had served the Saṅgha, then my zeal was made very strong.

**so imāya velāya Jambudīpamhi amissā devā samānehi manussehi, so idāni missaṃ katā**  
At that time in Jambudīpa, the heavenly beings (*deva*) were not mixed with mankind (*manussa*). Now they have been made to mixed.

**Parakkamassa eso phalaṃ**  
This is the fruit of effort (*parakkama*).

**Na hi idaṃ mahantena iva sakkonti pappontuṃ**  
This is not attained by a great being (*mahanta*) alone.

**khuddakena api parakkamena vipulaṃ api sakkonti saggaṃ ārādhetuṃ iva**  
Even by small effort (*khuddaka parakkama*), one can attain abundant heaven (*sagga*), one can be more favourably inclined.

**Etāya ca atthāya idaṃ sāvaneṃ**  
For this purpose this proclamation has been made:

**khuddakā ca uḷārā ca parakkameyyaṃ**  
Let both the small (*khuddaka*) and the eminent (*uḷāra*) exert themselves.

**iti Antā api jāneyyuṃ**  
Thus even those beyond the borders (*anta*) should know this.

**ciraṭhitiko ca parakkamaṃ hontu**  
Let effort be long-lasting (*ciraṭṭhitika*).

**Idaṃ ca atthaṃ vaḍḍhissati vipulā ca vaḍḍhissati**  
This teaching (*attha*) will grow and prosper, becoming abundant (*vipula*),

**Paṭidinaṃ api ca vaḍḍhissati iti**  
and it will continue to increase; day by day (*paṭidina*) it will keep growing in this way.

---
